Indoor Sound Visualization

Welcome to our educational Indoor Sound Visualion tool. The black dot in the rectanlge represents an omni-directional audio source, while the rectangle represents an environemt that the sound can reflect off. You can change the amplitude of the sound wave with the leftmost slider, which roughly corrseponds to the "loudness" of the sound. You can also manipulate the frequency of the sound wave with the middle slider, which corresponds to the "pitch" of the sound. Lastly, you can choose which material you would like the environment to be made of, in order to simulate different reflections of the sound waves at different locations.

Amplitude

The "loudness" of a sound wave is measured in decibels, which are measured as 10 times the logarithm of the Power Ratio. The Power Ratio is the Amplitude squared, so the "loudness" of the sound is 10 * log ( amplitude ^ 2). Here we have simplified this scientific equation so that the amplitude is directly related to the "loudness" of the sound, in order to more easily understand the amplitude's relation to the "loudness". This is visually represented as the thickness of the rings.
